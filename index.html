How this was built

Transparency matters. Below is exactly what the impact score measures, what it doesn't, and where its honest limitations lie.

The data

Every IPL ball-by-ball record from Cricsheet — the same data used by professional analysts and academic research. 1,202 matches, 285,907 deliveries, 798 unique players. Coverage runs from the inaugural 2007/08 season through the current 2026 campaign.

The impact formula

For batters: (runs above expected) + (SR premium) + (win bonus), all scaled by stage weight (final = 3×) and opposition strength.

For bowlers: (runs below expected) + (wkts × 20) + (win bonus), same multipliers.

"Expected" means "what a league-average player would have done at that moment in that season." A 30 off 18 in 2009 is far more valuable than the same in 2024.

Captain rankings

We score: titles × 100 + knockout wins × 15 + win-rate edge × matches + stars developed × 30 + average teammate impact lift × matches.

"Stars developed" measures how many of a captain's teammates flourished under their leadership compared to their own career baseline.

What we don't measure

We do not measure dressing-room culture, mentorship that doesn't show up on the scoresheet, or off-field leadership. We don't capture the fielding save: only catches, stumpings, and run-outs (because that's what's recorded).

Captain assignments are 88% verified per match (the remaining ~290 matches, mostly from 2007/08, lacked a high-confidence captain in our records).
