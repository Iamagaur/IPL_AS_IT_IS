Why we rebuilt from scratch

Previous versions invented arbitrary baselines (SR of 130 and avg of 30) without data support, applied 25% trophy weights that systematically favoured MI and CSK players, had wrong title counts (Rohit: listed as 5, actually 6; Hardik: listed as 1, actually 5; Russell: listed as 3, actually 2), and tagged Rohit Sharma as an opener despite him batting at #4–6 for Deccan Chargers from 2008–2010.

This version uses the data's own game-state-aware impact metric — already computed from ball-by-ball win-probability shifts by cricsheet. It is win-weighted, longevity-scaled via sqrt(), knockout-clutch aware, and uses title efficiency (titles per season) instead of raw counts. All title figures verified from Wikipedia. Batting roles assigned from career batting position data, not name-based assumptions.

— HOW SCORES ARE BUILT
Four dimensions. One impact score.
45%
Impact Intensity
The core signal: the data's own game-state impact per innings (from ball-by-ball win-probability shifts), multiplied by career volume, a win-rate modifier, and a venue adjustment. Chinnaswamy (avg score ~180) inflates batting numbers — players who batted heavily there get a 5–6% deflation. Win modifier range: ±20% vs ±15% previously, making team success matter more.
25%
Career Depth
Square-root scaled innings count. Rewards sustained excellence without letting longevity alone dominate. A 200-innings veteran gets 1.41× the depth credit of a 100-innings player — not 2×. Prevents career-volume from burying peak impact.
18%
Knockout Clutch
Actual playoff performance: runs per knockout innings for batters, wicket density + appearances for bowlers. Capped at 15 KO innings so one standout postseason campaign doesn't write an entire GOAT story. Consistency in knockouts wins this category.
12%
Trophy Gravity
Efficiency first: titles won per season as a squad member. Winning 3 titles in 6 seasons scores higher than 4 in 16. Hard-capped at 750 points so no dynasty player can outscore a zero-title all-time great through team fortune alone.

— Appendix

The Full Formula

All variables from the cricsheet JSON. No external lookups except verified title counts.

Batter Impact Score

// Core: game-state-aware impact per innings × volume × win-modifier × venue-adj WIN_MOD = 0.80 + (wins / inns) × 0.60 ← range ~1.04–1.15, more sensitive VENUE_ADJ = 1.0 by default ← 0.935–0.98 for Chinnaswamy/Wankhede batters AB de Villiers: 0.950 (38% innings at Chinnaswamy, ~13% venue inflation) V Kohli: 0.935 (50% innings at Chinnaswamy). CH Gayle: 0.960. Maxwell: 0.960 CORE = bat_impact_per_inn × inns × WIN_MOD × VENUE_ADJ // Depth: sqrt-scaled for fairness across career lengths DEPTH = sqrt(inns) × 60 // Knockout: runs per KO inn + capped appearance bonus + KO win weighting KO_WIN_W = 0.75 + win_ratio × 0.50 ← KO value weighted by career W% KO = (KO_RPI × 1.8 + min(knockout_inns, 15) × 12) × KO_WIN_W // Trophy: efficiency first (pace of winning), hard cap 750pts SEASONS = clamp(inns / 14, 3, 18) TRPH = min(titles/SEASONS×1000 + titles×60, 750) SCORE = CORE×0.45 + DEPTH×0.25 + KO×0.18 + TRPH×0.12

The venue adjustment addresses a real bias: Chinnaswamy's 920m altitude and short 61m square boundaries produce IPL's highest average first-innings scores (~180 vs league avg ~163). AB de Villiers scored 38% of his career IPL runs there. The adjustment is conservative and research-backed — it drops AB from #1 to #2 on the list, with Pollard taking the top spot on the basis of comparable impact, significantly higher win rate (52% vs 44%), and greater trophy contribution.

Bowler Impact Score

// Identical structure to batters WIN_MOD = 0.85 + (wins / inns) × 0.5 CORE = bowl_impact_per_inn × inns × WIN_MOD DEPTH = sqrt(inns) × 50 // KO: capped appearances + wicket density bonus KO = min(knockout_inns, 18) × 18 + (wkts/inns) × 30 TRPH = min(titles/SEASONS×1000 + titles×60, 750) SCORE = CORE×0.45 + DEPTH×0.25 + KO×0.18 + TRPH×0.12

No 1.8× win-wicket multiplier. A wicket is a wicket. Win% already modulates the score lightly. The bowler rankings are driven by who consistently created the most impact per innings across their career, not by which team they won with.

Captain Score

// Win edge: performance above league baseline of 50% WIN_EDGE = max(0, win_pct − 50) × matches × 0.45 // Knockout conversion rate + finals appearances KO_CONV = knockout_win_pct × 1.5 + finals × 30 // Title efficiency: capped at 4-season minimum SEASONS = max(4, matches / 14) TITLE_EFF = titles × 300 + (titles / SEASONS) × 600 // Player development (avg_lift = mean improvement in player impact) DEV = avg_lift × (pct_positive_lift/100) × 30 EXP = sqrt(matches) × 14 SCORE = WIN_EDGE + KO_CONV + TITLE_EFF + DEV + EXP

The 4-season minimum on efficiency prevents a 1-season flash captain topping the list. Both Dhoni and Rohit have 5 titles as IPL captains (Rohit's 6th was under Gilchrist at Deccan). Their scores separate on knockout conversion and player development, not just trophies.

All-Rounder & Fielder

// All-rounder: combined impact × win-mod + depth + trophies AR_CORE = ar_impact × 1.08 ← neutral win assumption AR_DEPTH = sqrt(bat_inns + bow_inns) × 50 AR_TRPH = min(TRPH, 750) AR_SCORE = AR_CORE×0.50 + AR_DEPTH×0.25 + AR_TRPH×0.12 // Fielder: dismissal impact + volume + trophies FLD_CORE = fld_impact FLD_DEPTH = sqrt(inns) × 14 FLD_TRPH = min(TRPH, 600) FLD_SCORE = FLD_CORE×0.60 + FLD_DEPTH×0.26 + FLD_TRPH×0.14

All-rounders use ar_impact (combined bat + bowl contribution from the data). Fielders use fld_impact which already weights stumpings and run-outs above catches. Both are secondary categories where data granularity is lower — treat scores as indicative, not definitive.
