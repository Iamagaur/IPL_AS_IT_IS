— Appendix

The full formula

Exactly how each score is computed. No black boxes.

Batter GOAT score

BASE = era_adj_runs × (SR_venue_adj / 130)
WIN = BASE × (wins / inns) × 1.5
KO = ko_runs × ko_SR_factor × 3.0
TROPHY = titles × 500
SCORE = WIN×0.25 + KO×0.25 + TROPHY×0.30 + BASE×0.20
era_adj: 2022+ stats × 0.75 · venue_adj: high-scoring ground SR × 0.85

Trophy bonus alone: one title = +500 points, five titles = +2500. This ensures players who were part of winning teams always rank above those who accumulated stats on perennial mid-table clubs.

Captain GOAT score

TITLES = titles_as_captain × 600
EFFICIENCY = (titles / years_as_captain) × 400
KO_WIN = knockout_wins × 50
HEADTOHEAD = final_wins × 150
FORTRESS = away_wins_rival_den × 30
STARS = players_developed × 40
Efficiency rewards Rohit's 5 titles in 10 yrs vs Dhoni's 5 in 16 yrs

Head-to-head final wins, fortress wins (away at rival's home ground), and efficiency are all first-class metrics. A captain who won 5 titles in 10 years scores higher on efficiency than the same 5 titles in 16 years — all else equal.

Era difficulty multiplier

Seasons from 2022 onwards see batter-friendly pitch preparations, power-play rule adjustments, and flat decks producing higher scores. We apply a 0.75× multiplier to all batting stats from 2022–2026 and a 0.90× for 2019–2021, normalising for the inflationary environment.

Bowling stats from 2022+ receive a 1.15× bonus — taking wickets in the run-fest era is comparatively harder.

Venue normalisation

High-scoring venues (Chinnaswamy, Wankhede, Eden Gardens) inflate strike rates. We identify the top 4 run-scoring venues by historical average and apply a 0.85× SR adjustment for performances at those grounds.

This directly addresses the AB de Villiers problem: he played the majority of his innings at Chinnaswamy, which is the single highest-scoring IPL venue. His raw SR of 151.89 becomes ~138 after venue adjustment — still elite, but no longer artificially inflated.
