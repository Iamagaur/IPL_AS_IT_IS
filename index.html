Why we rebuilt this list

The earlier "Definitive Edition" of this project carried a heavy trophy weight (25%) and added bonuses for wins that systematically pushed Mumbai Indians and CSK players up the order — even when their underlying per-ball performance didn't justify it. Multi-team players (Warner, Hardik, Buttler, Gambhir) had their stats fragmented across franchises. Some hand-coded title counts were wrong (Rohit Sharma had 6 not 5; Hardik had 5 not 1; Russell had 2 not 3).

This edition strips out those biases. The score now leans 70%+ on what a player did with the bat or ball — runs above expectation, economy gap from baseline, wickets-per-innings — and only 12% on trophies. Career stats are stitched together across every team a player represented. Title counts are sourced from Wikipedia and cross-checked. Where the data is incomplete or noisy, we say so.

— THE FOUR PILLARS

How a player gets their score

55%
Per-ball impact
Runs above the role-and-era baseline (openers vs middle vs finishers; pre-2016 vs post-2020). Built on real per-season league averages, not hand-picked multipliers.
20%
Volume credit
Sqrt-scaled experience bonus. Rewards 200-innings veterans without letting longevity alone dominate over peak performers.
13%
Knockouts
Performance — not just appearance — in playoffs and finals. Knockout runs and wickets weighted, capped to prevent dominance by 1-2 hot streaks.
12%
Trophy share
Capped at 12%. A player gets a small bonus per IPL title won, with diminishing returns past three titles. Team success matters; it does not define an individual.

— APPENDIX

The full formula

No black boxes. Below is exactly how each score is computed. You can replicate it from the JSON yourself.

Batter score

// Role baselines (set from league medians)
BASE_SR = opener:130 | middle:135 | finisher:148
BASE_AVG = opener:28 | middle:30 | finisher:25

// Era multiplier from career era (innings span)
ERA = 1.00 (≥130 inns), 0.94 (80-129), 0.88 (40-79), 0.82 (<40)

// Per-ball impact
SR_IMPACT = (SR − BASE_SR) × balls / 100
AVG_IMPACT = (avg − BASE_AVG) × dismissals / 6
CORE = (SR_IMPACT + AVG_IMPACT) × ERA
// Finishers: not-out bonus (closing the chase = role)
+ FIN_NO = (not_outs/inns) × runs × 0.25 × ERA

// Volume + KO + Trophy
VOL = sqrt(inns) × 30
KO = ko_runs × 1.5 + ko_inns × 12
TRPH = min(titles, 5) × 250

SCORE = CORE×0.55 + VOL×0.20 + KO×0.13 + TRPH×0.12
Trophy is capped — beyond 5 titles no extra weight is given.

The core idea: a player is rewarded for exceeding the baseline expected of their role, not for raw runs alone. An opener with SR 145 over 3,000 balls has an SR_IMPACT of +450; one with SR 125 over the same balls has −150. Era multiplier prevents post-2020 inflation from rewriting history.

Bowler score

// Baselines from league average
BASE_ECON = 8.30 (post-2020), 8.00 (2016-20), 7.70 (pre-2016)
BASE_SR = 22 balls per wicket

// Per-ball gap from baseline
ECON_IMPACT = (BASE_ECON − econ) × overs
WKT_IMPACT = wkts × 18
SR_BONUS = max(0, BASE_SR − bowl_SR) × wkts × 0.4
CORE = ECON_IMPACT + WKT_IMPACT + SR_BONUS

VOL = sqrt(inns) × 25
KO = ko_inns × 28
TRPH = min(titles, 5) × 200

SCORE = CORE×0.55 + VOL×0.20 + KO×0.13 + TRPH×0.12
No 1.8× win-wicket multiplier. A wicket is a wicket.

We removed the previous edition's controversial 1.8× multiplier on wickets-in-wins, which heavily favoured bowlers in dominant teams. A bowler who took 5/12 in a losing cause did the same skilled work as one who took 5/12 in a winning cause — the team result is largely outside their control.

Captain score

TITLES = titles × 400
EFFICIENCY = (titles / max(seasons_est, 4)) × 300
// 4-season minimum prevents 1-season fluke captains topping the list
EXPERIENCE = sqrt(matches) × 12
WIN_EDGE = max(0, win_pct − 50) × matches × 0.4

SCORE = TITLES + EFFICIENCY + EXPERIENCE + WIN_EDGE
Knockout-win % included where data exists.

A 4-season minimum on the efficiency divisor prevents short-stint captains (e.g. Patidar, Iyer) from topping the list off one championship. Both Rohit and Dhoni have five titles and long careers; their scores are very close — and that is honest.

Era difficulty & data caveats

Players whose careers were almost entirely post-2020 face a mild discount (era multiplier 0.82–0.88). Pre-2016 players score at full weight 1.0. The cutoffs are based on innings count as a proxy for career era, calibrated against actual season averages from the cricsheet data.

Two known data limits in the underlying JSON: (1) some multi-team players (notably JC Buttler) appear to have at least one team's spell missing from the per-team breakdown, though their overall totals look complete; (2) all-rounder cross-product impact is approximated since the data doesn't include true per-innings contribution. Where the data is uncertain, the displayed score range is intentionally narrow.
