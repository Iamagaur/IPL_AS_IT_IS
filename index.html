The five pillars of the Definitive methodology

25%
Trophy contribution
Titles are the only real currency. Each title a player won adds a significant bonus — but we've calibrated it at 25% so peak performers who never had winning sides aren't entirely buried. Five titles = massive advantage.
Weight: 25% of GOAT score
25%
Knockout performance
Playoffs and finals are where legacies are made. Knockout innings, runs, wickets and appearances are weighted 3× league games. Consistency in knockouts (multiple appearances) rewards the players who kept showing up.
Weight: 25% of GOAT score
30%
Role-aware win contribution
Openers face more balls and should score more — they get a higher run baseline. Middle-order batters are judged on average and strike rate efficiency. Finishers (No.6-8) are rewarded for not-outs and death-overs SR. Wickets in wins get a 1.8× premium for bowlers.
Weight: 30% of GOAT score
20%
Per-season era + venue
Post-2020 batting is discounted 30% — flat decks, smaller grounds, better bats. 2016–2020 gets 15% discount. Pre-2016 is baseline. Chinnaswamy/Wankhede SR gets a 15% haircut. This is why recent-era openers don't dominate the list.
Weight: 20% of GOAT score

— Appendix

The full formula

Exactly how each score is computed. No black boxes.

Batter GOAT score — role-aware

ERA_MULT = 1.0 (pre-2016) | 0.85 (2016-20) | 0.70 (post-2020)
ROLE_BASE:
  opener → runs × era × (SR_venueAdj / 125)
  middle → runs × era × (avg / 25) × (SR / 130)
  finisher → runs × era × (notOut_bonus) × (SR / 145)
WIN_CONTRIB = ROLE_BASE × (wins/inns) × 1.6
KO = ko_runs × 3.0 + ko_inns × 25
TROPHY = titles × 450
SCORE = WIN×0.30 + KO×0.25 + TROPHY×0.25 + ROLE_BASE×0.20
Venue adj: RCB = 0.85×SR | MI, KKR = 0.92×SR

The key innovation: openers compete against opener baselines (SR/125), middle-order against middle-order baselines (avg×SR), and finishers get credited for not-outs and death-SR. Rohit and Kohli now rank properly because their volume is recognised at an opener's baseline, not penalised for it.

Bowler GOAT score — win-wicket premium

ERA_MULT = 1.0 (pre-2016) | 1.08 (2016-20) | 1.18 (post-2020)
ADJ_WKTS = wkts × era_mult
ECON_FACTOR = max(0, (9.5 - econ) / 9.5)
BASE = adj_wkts × 65 × (1 + econ_factor)
WIN_WKTS = BASE × (wins/inns) × 1.8 ← key premium
KO = ko_inns × 90
TROPHY = titles × 450
SCORE = WIN_WKTS×0.30 + KO×0.25 + TROPHY×0.25 + BASE×0.20
1.8× multiplier on wickets taken in winning matches

The 1.8× win-wicket multiplier is the core change. Bumrah's 77 wins in 152 innings (50.6% win rate) gets fully rewarded. Malinga and Bravo — both in MI's five-title dynasty — now appear near the top of the list where they belong.

Era difficulty per innings band

Rather than a crude binary (pre/post 2022), we use innings count as a career-span proxy across four IPL eras. Players who played mostly in the early IPL (big innings count, older data) score at baseline 1.0. Mid-era players get slight adjustments. Recent-era heavy players face the strongest discount.

Post-2020 batting gets a 0.70× multiplier — the most severe penalty — because this era saw the highest ever T20 scores globally. Bowling in post-2020 gets 1.18× bonus for the same reason.

Captain GOAT score

TITLES = titles × 600
EFFICIENCY = (titles / seasons_est) × 500
WIN_EDGE = max(0, win_pct − 50) × matches × 0.5
KO_WIN = knockout_win_pct × 12
H2H = final_wins × 150
Efficiency = titles per ~14-match season. Rewards pace of winning.

Rohit Sharma leads captains decisively: 5 titles in 10 seasons (0.50/yr) vs Dhoni's 5 in 16 (0.31/yr). Rohit's MI won 3 finals directly against Dhoni's CSK. The efficiency metric was designed specifically to separate these two and it does.
